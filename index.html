About

I don't fit in one box.
That's the point.

Most people who've spent 15 years in data end up deep in one lane — pure engineering, pure analytics, or pure management. I went a different direction. I've architected enterprise data warehouses, led healthcare analytics functions built from scratch, managed enterprise client relationships at the CIO and CMO level, and driven complex technology implementations across high-growth SaaS organizations. I've done all of it hands-on, and I've done all of it in the notoriously complex world of healthcare.

What connects those experiences isn't a job title — it's a belief that data is only as valuable as the clarity it creates. I've spent my career making sure the right people had the right information to make the right calls: whether that was a health system CIO rethinking their data strategy, a payer stakeholder reviewing ACO performance, or an engineering team that needed someone to translate business reality into architecture decisions.

I lead as a player-coach. I build teams, but I stay close to the work. I set direction, but I'm comfortable getting into the SQL, the data model, or the client meeting when that's what the situation demands. I don't delegate understanding — I earn it, and then I use it to make my team and my organization better.
